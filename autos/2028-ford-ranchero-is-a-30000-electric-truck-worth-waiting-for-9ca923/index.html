2028 Ford Ranchero Is a $30,000 Electric Truck Worth Waiting For

What's happening

Ford is developing an electric pickup truck expected to launch around 2028 with a starting price near $30,000. While the automaker hasn't officially confirmed the vehicle's name or full specifications, early indications suggest it could be a game-changing entry in the EV truck market.

Who's involved

Ford Motor Company, one of America's largest automakers with a storied history in truck manufacturing, is spearheading this project. The company has been aggressively expanding its electric vehicle lineup to compete with Tesla and other EV makers.

Why it matters

A $30,000 electric truck could democratize EV ownership by making electric vehicles accessible to mainstream truck buyers who've traditionally been priced out of the EV market. This move positions Ford to capture a significant share of the growing electric truck segment and maintain its dominance in the truck category it has long dominated with gas-powered vehicles.

More context

Ford has invested heavily in EV development over the past five years, launching vehicles like the Mustang Mach-E and F-150 Lightning to compete in the electric market. The truck segment remains critical to Ford's profitability and brand identity, making an affordable EV option strategically important. Competitors like Tesla (Cybertruck) and Chevrolet (Silverado EV) are already moving into this space, intensifying pressure on Ford. The $30,000 price point would undercut most current electric truck offerings, potentially reshaping market expectations around EV pricing.
