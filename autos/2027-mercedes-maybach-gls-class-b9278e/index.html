2027 Mercedes-Maybach GLS-Class

What's happening

Mercedes-Benz is developing a Maybach version of its GLS-Class luxury SUV set to debut in 2027. This ultra-premium variant will sit at the top of the automaker's SUV lineup, combining the Maybach brand's exclusivity with the practical size of an SUV.

Who's involved

Mercedes-Benz, the German luxury automaker, is behind this project through its Maybach sub-brand, which specializes in ultra-high-end vehicles positioned above the standard Mercedes offerings. Maybach has historically focused on sedans but is expanding into the SUV segment to capture growing demand.

Why it matters

This launch signals how luxury automakers are shifting toward SUVs as their flagship models, even for prestigious sub-brands typically associated with sedans. The Maybach GLS will compete directly with vehicles like the Rolls-Royce Cullinan and BMW's high-end X7, capturing wealthy buyers who prioritize size and presence over traditional sedan formats.

More context

Maybach was revived by Mercedes-Benz in 2021 after a 12-year hiatus, initially with the S-Class sedan as its only offering. The SUV market has dramatically reshaped luxury vehicle sales, with premium brands increasingly developing SUV models to meet consumer preferences for elevated driving positions and cargo space. Mercedes' current GLS-Class is already one of the brand's most popular models, and the Maybach variant will offer enhanced exclusivity, bespoke interiors, and performance tuning to justify premium pricing in the already-crowded ultra-luxury SUV segment.
