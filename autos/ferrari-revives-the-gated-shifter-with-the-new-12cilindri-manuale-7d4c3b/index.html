Ferrari Revives the Gated Shifter with the New 12Cilindri Manuale

What's happening

Ferrari is introducing a simulated manual transmission in its new 12Cilindri Manuale, featuring a traditional gated shifter that creates the tactile experience of a manual without any physical mechanical connection to the engine. The car delivers the mechanical feel and engagement drivers crave while using modern automatic transmission technology underneath.

Who's involved

Ferrari, the iconic Italian luxury automaker known for high-performance sports cars, is behind this innovation. The company has a storied history of manual transmissions but has largely shifted to automatics in recent decades due to performance and efficiency demands.

Why it matters

This move signals Ferrari's attempt to preserve the emotional connection drivers have with manual transmissions while acknowledging that modern performance and emissions standards make traditional manuals impractical. For enthusiasts, it offers the last chance to experience gated shifter engagement in a new Ferrari, potentially becoming a highly collectible future classic.

More context

Manual transmissions have nearly disappeared from high-performance cars as dual-clutch and automatic transmissions outperform them in acceleration and fuel efficiency. Ferrari phased out traditional manuals years ago, but enthusiasts have consistently lamented their loss. This simulated manual approach—similar to video game force-feedback controllers—allows Ferrari to offer nostalgia and engagement without sacrificing the 12Cilindri's modern performance capabilities. The gated shifter, a mechanical component that guides gear selection through defined positions, remains an iconic element of classic Ferrari DNA.
