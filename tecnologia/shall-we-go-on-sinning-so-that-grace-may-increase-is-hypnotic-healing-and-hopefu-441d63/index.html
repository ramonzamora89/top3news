Shall We Go On Sinning So That Grace May Increase? is hypnotic, healing, and hopeful

What's happening

Drew Daniel of experimental electronic duo Matmos has released a new project titled 'Shall We Go On Sinning So That Grace May Increase?' that critics are describing as hypnotic, healing, and hopeful. The work showcases Daniel's signature sound while exploring introspective and emotional territories.

Who's involved

Drew Daniel is one half of Matmos, an acclaimed experimental electronic music duo known for their innovative production work and solo records. The pair has collaborated extensively with major artists, most notably producing Bjork's landmark album Vespertine, and have built a reputation for pushing genre boundaries in contemporary music.

Why it matters

This release demonstrates the continued artistic evolution of one of experimental music's most respected figures and adds to Matmos' cultural influence in electronic music. Daniel's prolific output across multiple projects reinforces the duo's significance in shaping contemporary experimental and art music.

More context

Matmos established themselves as innovative producers through critically acclaimed solo work, including Daniel's masterpiece 'A Chance to Cut Is a Chance to Cure,' and their transformative production on Bjork's 'Vespertine,' which became a defining record of 2000s art-pop. Drew Daniel is known for his ferociously prolific creative process, constantly working on multiple projects simultaneously. The experimental electronic music landscape has increasingly valued the kind of introspective, emotionally intelligent approach that characterizes recent Matmos work, moving the genre beyond purely technical showcases.
