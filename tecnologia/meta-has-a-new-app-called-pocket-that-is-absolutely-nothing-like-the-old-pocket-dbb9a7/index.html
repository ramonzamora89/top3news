Meta has a new app called Pocket that is absolutely nothing like the old Pocket

What's happening

Meta is launching a new app called Pocket that lets users create and share interactive AI-generated "gizmos" built from simple prompts. The app bears the same name as Mozilla's beloved read-it-later service, but serves an entirely different purpose.

Who's involved

Meta, the social media and technology giant owned by Mark Zuckerberg, is behind this new app launch. Mozilla previously operated the original Pocket app, a popular read-it-later tool that helped users save and organize articles and content.

Why it matters

The launch signals Meta's aggressive push into AI-powered creative tools and generative features across its product ecosystem. The name reuse highlights how quickly the tech landscape shifts—what was once a standalone product category (read-it-later apps) is now being replaced by AI-focused applications.

More context

Mozilla shut down the original Pocket app last year, leaving a gap in the read-it-later market where it had built a loyal user base over years of operation. Meta has been rapidly integrating AI capabilities into its platforms, from generative image tools to AI chatbots. The "Pocket" name carries brand recognition from its previous incarnation, though Meta's version targets an entirely different use case. This repositioning reflects the broader industry shift away from traditional productivity tools toward AI-assisted content creation and sharing.
