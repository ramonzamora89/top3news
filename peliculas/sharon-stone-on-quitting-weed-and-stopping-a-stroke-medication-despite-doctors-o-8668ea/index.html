Sharon Stone on Quitting Weed and Stopping a Stroke Medication Despite Doctors’ Orders: ‘It Was Like Going Off of F—ing Heroin’

What's happening

Sharon Stone has quit smoking weed and recently stopped taking one of her stroke medications against her doctors' advice. The actress describes the experience of quitting cannabis as being comparable to withdrawing from heroin.

Who's involved

Sharon Stone is a renowned actress and stroke survivor who suffered a near-fatal stroke in 2001 that required long-term medical management and medication. She has been open about her health struggles and recovery journey in recent years.

Why it matters

Stone's public disclosure about medication non-compliance and substance use highlights the challenges patients face in managing post-stroke recovery and addiction-like dependencies on both prescription drugs and cannabis. Her experience raises questions about patient autonomy versus medical guidance and the difficulty of navigating long-term health management after major medical events.

More context

Stone suffered a near-fatal stroke in 2001 that significantly impacted her health and career. Following the stroke, she was prescribed multiple medications to prevent future incidents and manage her condition. Stone stopped one of these stroke medications in September (of the prior year) despite medical advice against doing so. The actress has become increasingly vocal about her health journey and personal struggles, using her platform to discuss topics like recovery, addiction, and medical decision-making.
