MPA, APSA Reopen Film Fund for Asia Pacific Screenwriters – Global Bulletin

What's happening

The Motion Picture Association and Asia Pacific Screen Awards have reopened their joint film fund, now accepting applications for 2026. The initiative will distribute five $10,000 grants to screenwriters developing scripts in the Asia Pacific region.

Who's involved

The Motion Picture Association (MPA), the trade organization representing major Hollywood studios, is partnering with the Asia Pacific Screen Awards (APSA), the region's premier film organization. Together they're targeting emerging and established screenwriters across the Asia Pacific territory.

Why it matters

The fund directly supports script development in a region increasingly vital to global cinema, helping underfinanced writers bring projects to life. This investment can unlock productions that might otherwise struggle to secure early-stage financing, potentially bringing fresh Asia Pacific voices to international audiences.

More context

The MPA APSA Academy Film Fund represents ongoing industry efforts to nurture screenwriting talent outside traditional Western markets as Asia Pacific becomes a major production hub. The fund has operated previously, suggesting this reopening reflects sustained commitment to regional talent development. Script development funding is critical in the Asia Pacific, where financing mechanisms often lag behind North American and European markets. The $10,000 grants, while modest, serve as crucial seed money for writers to complete drafts that can attract larger production investments.
