Daniel Wu on ‘Cold War 1994,’ ‘Twilight of the Warriors’ and Coming Home to Hong Kong: ‘The Craft Still Challenges Me’

What's happening

Daniel Wu received the Best from the East Award at the New York Asian Film Festival, marking a major comeback moment for the Hong Kong actor. He's simultaneously anchoring two of Hong Kong cinema's biggest franchises: "Cold War 1994" (the third installment) and the "Twilight of the Warriors" series.

Who's involved

Daniel Wu is a Hong Kong-American actor who has built a significant career spanning Hollywood and Asian cinema, known for roles in "Into the Badlands" and various action films. The New York Asian Film Festival is a prestigious venue celebrating films from across Asia.

Why it matters

Wu's central positioning in Hong Kong's two major franchises signals a revitalization of the actor's career in his home market after years of primarily working in international productions. This recognition underscores Hong Kong cinema's continued global relevance and the festival's role in amplifying Asian storytelling to international audiences.

More context

Hong Kong's film industry experienced a significant decline after the 2019 protests and subsequent political changes, with many productions and talent relocating. The "Cold War" franchise and "Twilight of the Warriors" represent major studio efforts to revive the territory's action cinema heritage. Wu's return to prominent Hong Kong roles after focusing on Hollywood projects represents both a personal and industry shift, as major stars increasingly balance international work with homegrown productions. The Best from the East Award specifically honors excellence in Asian filmmaking, making this recognition particularly meaningful for Hong Kong's film ecosystem.
