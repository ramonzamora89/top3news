Celina Vikram Jaitly Returns to the Big Screen as Sister Nibedita, the Irish-Born Disciple of Swami Vivekananda (EXCLUSIVE)

What's happening

Actor Celina Vikram Jaitly is returning to feature films to play Sister Nibedita, the Irish-born disciple of Swami Vivekananda, in a biographical drama. Director Ram Kamal Mukherjee is helming the project, reuniting with Jaitly after their previous collaboration on 'Season's Greetings.'

Who's involved

Celina Vikram Jaitly is an Indian actor making a comeback to cinema after a hiatus. Ram Kamal Mukherjee is a film director known for biographical and drama work who has previously worked with Jaitly.

Why it matters

Jaitly's return marks a significant moment for an actor stepping back into the feature film space after time away. The project brings attention to Sister Nibedita's historical significance as a Western follower of Swami Vivekananda, potentially broadening audience awareness of this lesser-known spiritual figure.

More context

Sister Nibedita (born Margaret Elizabeth Noble) was an Irish-born social activist and disciple of Swami Vivekananda in late 19th and early 20th century India. Biographical dramas centered on spiritual and historical figures have gained traction in Indian cinema in recent years. Jaitly and Mukherjee's previous collaboration on 'Season's Greetings' established creative rapport between the actor and director. The film represents a specific casting choice to portray the Irish origins of this cross-cultural spiritual figure.
