‘Murciélago’ Manga Gets 2027 TV Anime Adaptation on HIDIVE

What's happening

Yoshimura Kana's manga 'Murciélago' is being adapted into a TV anime premiering in 2027. HIDIVE has secured exclusive streaming rights for the U.S., Canada, and several other markets, with the announcement made at Anime Expo 2026.

Who's involved

HIDIVE is a prominent anime streaming platform known for licensing and distributing Japanese animation content globally. Yoshimura Kana is the manga creator, with the original work serialized through Square Enix, a major Japanese publisher and entertainment company.

Why it matters

This adaptation expands 'Murciélaco's reach from printed manga to a broader anime audience across multiple markets. The 2027 premiere adds to HIDIVE's growing anime catalog and demonstrates continued investment in adapting manga properties into animated series.

More context

'Murciélago' is serialized in a Square Enix publication, placing it within one of Japan's largest manga publishing ecosystems. Anime Expo 2026 served as the official venue for this announcement, reflecting how major anime industry news is increasingly unveiled at dedicated conventions. The exclusive licensing deal with HIDIVE highlights how streaming platforms compete aggressively for anime distribution rights. Manga-to-anime adaptations remain a core industry strategy, with studios continuously developing new properties to meet international demand.
