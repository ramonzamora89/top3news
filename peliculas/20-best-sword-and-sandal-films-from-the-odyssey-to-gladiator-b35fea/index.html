20 Best Sword-and-Sandal Films, From ‘The Odyssey’ to ‘Gladiator’

What's happening

Variety has published a ranking of the 20 best sword-and-sandal films, highlighting Christopher Nolan's new take on Homer's "The Odyssey" as a major reinvigoration of the genre. The piece suggests this high-profile project is reviving interest in a film category that has seen minimal major releases in recent decades.

Who's involved

Christopher Nolan, the acclaimed director known for complex blockbusters like "Inception" and "Oppenheimer," is helming the Odyssey adaptation. Variety, a major entertainment trade publication, is curating the definitive list of films in this classical historical genre.

Why it matters

A Nolan-helmed sword-and-sandal epic signals major studio confidence in reviving a dormant genre, potentially triggering a wave of similar historical epics in development. This could reshape streaming and theatrical release strategies for prestige historical content over the next few years.

More context

The sword-and-sandal genre—featuring ancient Roman, Greek, and Mediterranean historical settings—peaked in the 1950s-60s with films like "Ben-Hur" and "Spartacus," then experienced a modest revival in the 2000s with "Gladiator" (2000) and "Troy" (2004). The genre has largely stagnated since then, with few major theatrical releases attempting the expensive historical epic format. Nolan's involvement, combined with his proven track record of delivering both critical and commercial successes, carries significant weight in Hollywood's investment decisions.
