Box Office: ‘Moana’ Flounders With $43 Million Debut, Among Weakest of Disney’s Live-Action Remakes

What's happening

Disney's live-action 'Moana' remake opened with just $43 million domestically, claiming the No. 1 spot but marking one of the studio's weakest live-action remake debuts. The $250 million production faced a disappointing box office performance despite its high profile and significant budget.

Who's involved

Disney, the entertainment giant behind the film, produced the live-action adaptation of its 2016 animated musical. The studio has built a franchise on remaking its classic animated properties into live-action films in recent years.

Why it matters

The weak debut signals potential audience fatigue with Disney's live-action remake formula and raises questions about the film's ability to recoup its massive production budget globally. It suggests that even major Disney intellectual property may not guarantee box office success, forcing the studio to reassess its strategy for future adaptations.

More context

Disney has increasingly relied on live-action remakes of animated classics as a core business strategy, with projects like 'The Lion King,' 'Beauty and the Beast,' and 'Cinderella' achieving varying degrees of success. The $43 million opening is notably lower than other Disney live-action debuts, reflecting either changing audience preferences or market saturation with similar projects. 'Moana' carried particular weight as the studio's attempt to capitalize on the 2016 animated film's cultural impact and popular soundtrack. This performance comes amid broader industry challenges, including post-pandemic theatrical attendance patterns and increased competition from streaming platforms.
