New Order’s Gillian Gilbert, Stephen Morris Not Touring With Band ‘Due to Personal Health Reasons’

What's happening

New Order keyboardist Gillian Gilbert and drummer Stephen Morris won't tour with the band for the foreseeable future due to personal health reasons. The two members are sidelined from an upcoming show in Chile and beyond.

Who's involved

New Order is an iconic British electronic rock band formed in 1980 from the remnants of Joy Division. Gilbert and Morris have been core members, with Gilbert on keyboards and Morris on drums since the band's inception.

Why it matters

The absence of two founding members significantly impacts New Order's live performances and touring schedule, forcing the band to adjust its plans. This raises questions about the band's long-term viability as a touring entity and whether additional dates will be postponed or cancelled.

More context

New Order has maintained an active touring schedule in recent years following their 2015 reunion and 2020 album 'Music Complete' reissue. The band is one of the most influential electronic acts in rock history, known for hits like 'Blue Monday' and 'Bizarre Love Triangle.' This marks a significant disruption to their plans, particularly as they had scheduled international dates including the Chile appearance. The band has not specified the nature of the health issues or provided a timeline for the members' return.
