Rush Postpones 2 Shows After Geddy Lee Diagnosed With Laryngitis and Bronchitis

What's happening

Rush has postponed two shows scheduled at Dickies Arena in Fort Worth, Texas. The postponement comes after frontman Geddy Lee was diagnosed with laryngitis and bronchitis.

Who's involved

Rush is a legendary Canadian rock band fronted by vocalist and bassist Geddy Lee, alongside drummer Neil Peart and guitarist Alex Lifeson. The trio has been performing together for decades and remains one of rock music's most influential acts.

Why it matters

The postponement affects fans who had tickets to the Fort Worth dates and impacts the band's touring schedule. For a vocalist-dependent performance like Rush's, laryngitis and bronchitis directly compromise the ability to deliver the high-energy show fans expect, making the postponement a necessary health decision.

More context

Rush has maintained an active touring schedule in recent years despite the band members being in their 70s. Geddy Lee's voice is central to Rush's identity, known for his distinctive high tenor range and technical vocal delivery across the band's catalog spanning five decades. The Dickies Arena shows were part of what would be among the band's final touring dates as they continue performing selectively. Health issues affecting touring musicians have become increasingly common, with artists prioritizing recovery to ensure quality performances rather than pushing through illness.
