Jim Gilstrap, ‘Good Times’ Singer and Vocalist for Stevie Wonder and Others, Dead at 79

What's happening

Jim Gilstrap, the legendary session vocalist behind the iconic 'Good Times' theme song, has died at 79. The prolific backup singer lent his voice to countless hit recordings across multiple decades.

Who's involved

Jim Gilstrap was a session vocalist who worked with some of music's biggest names including Stevie Wonder, Whitney Houston, Michael Jackson, and Harry Styles. He became best known for performing the theme song to the 1970s sitcom 'Good Times.'

Why it matters

Gilstrap's death marks the loss of a foundational figure in music production whose uncredited work shaped the sound of popular music from the '70s onward. His contributions highlight the often-invisible role session singers play in creating the hits that define generations of music.

More context

Gilstrap's career exemplified the golden age of session musicianship, a practice where specialized vocalists were hired to provide backup, harmonies, or featured vocals on recordings without receiving prominent credit or royalties. The 'Good Times' theme became one of his most recognizable works, introducing his voice to millions of television viewers weekly. Beyond television, his session work spanned soul, pop, R&B, and rock across multiple eras. The decline of traditional session work in recent decades has made figures like Gilstrap increasingly rare in the modern music industry.
