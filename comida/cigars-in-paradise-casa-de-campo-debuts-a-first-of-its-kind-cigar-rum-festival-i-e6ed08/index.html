Cigars in Paradise: Casa de Campo Debuts a First-of-Its-Kind Cigar & Rum Festival in the Dominican Republic

What's happening

Casa de Campo is launching Cigars in Paradise, a first-of-its-kind multi-day festival in the Dominican Republic that combines cigar and rum experiences. The event features tastings, master sessions, and luxury resort experiences for attendees.

Who's involved

Casa de Campo, a luxury resort destination in the Dominican Republic, is organizing the festival. The resort is known for its high-end accommodations and is capitalizing on the country's heritage as a major cigar and rum producer.

Why it matters

The festival positions the Dominican Republic as a premier destination for premium tobacco and spirits tourism, potentially attracting affluent travelers seeking experiential luxury getaways. This could boost the region's tourism economy and elevate the profile of Dominican cigar and rum brands globally.

More context

The Dominican Republic is the world's largest cigar exporter and has a centuries-old tradition of tobacco cultivation and cigar production. The country is also renowned for its rum industry, with brands like Bacardi and Brugal having strong heritage ties to the island. Festival tourism focused on local products and craftsmanship has grown significantly post-pandemic, as luxury travelers seek authentic, immersive experiences. This debut event represents a strategic convergence of two of the Dominican Republic's most prestigious industries.
